Questions and Answers

Why Maintaining Your Culture Matters?

Culture is the heart of who we are. It shapes how we tell stories, celebrate, remember our history, and imagine the future. Staying connected to your culture keeps you grounded and confident.

  • Identity: Culture helps you understand who you are and where you come from.
  • Community: Traditions, festivals, and gatherings strengthen bonds and create a sense of belonging.
  • History: Maintaining culture passes on lessons, wisdom, and stories from ancestors.
  • Diversity: Embracing your culture adds richness to society and promotes understanding.
  • Strength: Culture provides comfort, guidance, and hope during challenging times.

Why Some Pacific Teens Step Away From Their Culture?

Many Pacific teenagers face pressures that make them distance themselves from their culture. Understanding these reasons can help families and communities provide better support.

  • Modern influences: Movies, music, social media, and Western lifestyles can make cultural practices feel old-fashioned or less popular.
  • Peer pressure: Teens may hide their culture to fit in with friends who don’t practice it or speak the language.
  • Balancing two worlds: Traditional expectations at home versus modern expectations at school or in the community can be confusing and stressful.
  • Lack of cultural knowledge: Some teens weren’t taught their language, customs, or history growing up, leaving them disconnected.
  • Negative stereotypes: Being teased or judged for being Pacific can make teens avoid cultural expression.

Living Between Multiple Cultures

Growing up with two or more cultures can be empowering but also challenging. Many young people feel pulled in different directions and struggle to meet expectations from each culture.

  • Identity conflict: Teens may feel they do not fully belong anywhere.
  • Pressure to comply: Each culture has its own traditions and expectations, which can create stress.
  • Balancing act: Navigating family, community, and society expectations can be overwhelming.
  • Support matters: Recognizing these challenges helps teens feel proud, confident, and supported in all parts of who they are.
